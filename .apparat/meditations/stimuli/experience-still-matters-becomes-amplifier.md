# Experience Still Matters — and Becomes an Amplifier

Agents are amplifiers of existing skill and experience. The more you have, the more they multiply it.

25 years of engineering experience lets you: describe a problem in one precise sentence and know the agent will find and fix it. Know which problems are one-sentence prompts vs. which are genuinely open-ended. Use sophisticated technical language that the agent also knows — enabling high-bandwidth collaboration.

Without that background, you're guessing at how to describe problems. With it, you're directing at altitude.

The counterpoint: your intuitions about *how long things take* are now wrong. Not your judgment about *what to build* or *what's a hard problem* — just your time estimates. Recalibrate those. Keep everything else.

Non-programmers can now build things. But experienced engineers can build things that are correct, secure, maintainable, and architecturally sound — at a speed that compounds.
