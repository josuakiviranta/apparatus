# The Exhaustion Is Real (and Probably Temporary)

Working with agents at high intensity is genuinely exhausting in a way that wasn't expected. You get more done, you have more free time — and your brain is still drained.

The intensity of directing multiple agents, reviewing outputs, making rapid decisions, and maintaining quality across a higher volume of work is cognitively expensive. It's not the same kind of tired as manual coding, but it's real.

The working hypothesis: this is a novelty adaptation effect. As the workflows normalize and the patterns become automatic, the cognitive overhead will drop.

For now: notice it. Don't push through exhaustion as if it doesn't exist. The productivity gains are real, but they come with a cost. Factor that in.
